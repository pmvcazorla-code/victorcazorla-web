Academic Education

Education as a consequence

There is a way of being in the world that does not disappear with time; that of the person who perseveres in the question even when answers appear given. That curiosity, closer to the childlike gesture of dismantling what works than to the confidence of the expert who takes it for granted, leads to crossing disciplines, inhabiting different languages, and distrusting premature certainties. Education, therefore, is not the object of knowledge itself, but the inevitable effect of a gaze that refuses to get used to seeing the world as it is given to it.
